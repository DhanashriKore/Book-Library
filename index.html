World Famous Books

Mrityunjay

By Shivaji Sawant. A poignant retelling of Karna's life from the Mahabharata, exploring his struggles with identity, loyalty, and destiny.

Mahabharata

The Mahabharata is an ancient Indian epic centered on the great war between the Pandavas and Kauravas, highlighting themes of duty, righteousness, and destiny.

Ramayana

The Ramayana is an ancient Indian epic that narrates the journey of Prince Rama to rescue his wife Sita from the demon king Ravana, highlighting themes of duty, loyalty, and righteousness..

The God of small Things

By Arundhati Roy is a poignant novel that explores the lives of two twins in Kerala, delving into themes of caste, forbidden love, and societal norms, set against the backdrop of family tragedy.

1984

By George Orwell. A dystopian novel that delves into the dangers of totalitarianism.

To Kill a Mockingbird

By Harper Lee. A classic that explores racial injustice in the American South.

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone

By J.K. Rowling. The story of a young wizard and his adventures.

Pride and Prejudice

By Jane Austen. A timeless romance between Elizabeth Bennet and Mr. Darcy.

The Hobbit

By J.R.R. Tolkien. An epic fantasy of adventure and discovery.

Moby Dick

By Herman Melville. The tale of Captain Ahab's obsessive quest to kill the white whale.

Jadu

By Maharaj Sachidanand is a spiritual book that explores mysticism, inner transformation, and the power of divine experiences, guiding readers toward self-realization and spiritual awakening.

Think Like Monk

By Jay Shetty is a practical guide that combines ancient wisdom and modern psychology to help readers cultivate mindfulness, purpose, and positive habits for a fulfilling life.
